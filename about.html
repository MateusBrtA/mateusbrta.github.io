The Project

Universe Sim is a real-time interactive N-body gravitational simulator running entirely in your browser. It was built out of a passion for physics, space, and making science accessible and fun for everyone — from students to enthusiasts.

Every body in the simulation exerts gravitational force on every other body simultaneously. Planet sizes, orbital distances and masses are based on real astronomical data. Moons are placed within stable fractions of their parent planet's Hill sphere. Saturn and Uranus feature particle ring systems with independently orbiting particles following Keplerian dynamics.

What You Can Do

  • Explore the full Solar System — all planets, major moons, asteroid belt and Kuiper Belt Objects
  • Spawn new bodies: rocky planets, gas giants, stars, white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes, nebulae
  • Watch stellar evolution — transform stars into red giants, white dwarfs, neutron stars or black holes
  • Edit any body's mass, radius and temperature in real time and watch physics respond
  • Control simulation speed from 0× to 30× and observe orbital trail shapes evolve
  • Navigate freely through space in first-person free-fly mode
  • Visualize gravitational spacetime curvature with the deforming gravity grid

Technology

Universe Sim is built with Three.js for 3D WebGL rendering. There is no server — the entire physics engine, renderer and UI run locally on your device. The simulator uses a 3-substep integrator with softened gravity for numerical stability.

The project is hosted on GitHub Pages. No account, download or installation is ever required to use it.

Our Mission

We believe interactive simulation is one of the most powerful ways to understand the universe. By making orbital mechanics tangible and explorable, we hope to spark curiosity about astronomy, physics, and the true scale of space in anyone who visits.
